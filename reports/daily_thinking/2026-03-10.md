# Daily Thinking — 2026-03-10

Date: 2026-03-10
Audience: internal only
Status: working project diary

## What moved today

Today changed the project from a loose interpretability exploration into a more coherent
observability program.

The biggest shift was not a single metric. It was that several pieces started reinforcing one
another:

- read-only observation vs write-back intervention
- onset vs later expansion
- panel-level geometry vs entropy
- token-level onset vs post-onset persistence
- transition as ambiguity boundary rather than unexplained noise

The work now feels less like “finding interesting internal signals” and more like building a
disciplined internal science of regime change.

## Main steps and what they led to

### 1. Observer vs intervention boundary hardened

The read-only oscilloscope and the unified no-write-back baseline were effectively identical in
shared SAE subspace, while reconstruction / write-back produced strong trajectory distortion.

What this changed:

- write-back can no longer be treated as neutral observation
- read-only traces became the primary evidence surface
- the entire microscopy stack became epistemically cleaner

### 2. Trajectory block turned scattered intuitions into a structured picture

The first trajectory block supported three linked claims in GPT-2:

- geometry beat entropy at panel level
- `Layer 6` emerged as strongest local onset candidate
- regime fingerprints separated at regime level

What this changed:

- hallucination-prone behavior stopped looking like “just output error”
- the project gained a first real internal model of onset and regime structure

### 3. Dense sweep separated onset from expansion

The denser `5-12` sweep sharpened the picture:

- strongest local onset remained at `Layer 6`
- largest later expansion moved to `10 -> 11`

What this changed:

- onset and expansion can no longer be collapsed into one event
- `Layer 6` should be treated as local onset candidate, not total explanation

### 4. Expanded panel made the ambiguity boundary visible

The expanded panel preserved the main picture, but showed more clearly that `transition` is the weak
boundary.

What this changed:

- the signal became more trustworthy overall because it survived a harder panel
- but the project lost the right to talk as if geometry cleanly solved detection
- transition became a real research object in its own right

### 5. Lead-time introduced a second timescale

Token-level analysis showed:

- geometry delta still beat entropy delta overall
- a conservative threshold isolated a small hallucination-prone slice before prompt end
- broad thresholding was still non-specific

What this changed:

- the work gained a real, if narrow, pre-end signal
- the right language became “measurable slice” rather than “early-warning system”

### 6. Counter-case pass changed how transition should be read

The transition counter-case pass showed that `transition` detections are not best read as simple
duplicates of hallucination.

The strongest current difference was:

- hallucination-prone detections persist longer after onset
- transition detections decay faster and disappear under conservative thresholding

What this changed:

- persistence became more important than raw onset magnitude
- the best current internal model became:
  - geometry excursion
  - persistence filtering
  - hallucination-prone continuation

## Main conceptual shifts

### From scalar signal to staged process

Earlier in the work, it would have been easy to talk as if a single internal metric might “find
hallucination.”

That is no longer the right framing.

The better framing now is:

- `Stage 1`: onset-sensitive geometry excursion
- `Stage 2`: persistence or survival after onset
- `Stage 3`: hallucination-prone continuation

This is more modest than a detector claim, but more scientifically serious.

### From transition as nuisance to transition as boundary case

Transition is not just noise.

It is the place where the model shows onset-like behavior without necessarily stabilizing into the
same failure slice. That makes it methodologically valuable, because it exposes where geometry alone
stops being enough.

### From neuron-level intuition to higher-order dynamics

Reading around circuits, monosemanticity, and sparse coding also reinforced something that has been
emerging inside the work:

- single neurons are likely too poor an ontology
- SAE-like decomposition is useful, but not sufficient by itself
- trajectory, regime, onset, and persistence may be closer to the right analysis surface for the
  current problem

This does not prove a mechanism, but it clarifies why the work keeps moving away from simple local
unit stories.

## Hypotheses that strengthened today

- hallucination-prone behavior in the current GPT-2 setup is better described as a dynamic regime
  pattern than as entropy alone
- `Layer 6` is a defensible local onset candidate in the present setup
- `10 -> 11` is a defensible later expansion step in the present setup
- persistence is currently the best disambiguator between hallucination-prone and transition
  detections
- the ambiguity boundary is a core object of study, not an annoyance to be ignored

## Claim boundaries that tightened today

Allowed:

- geometry is more informative than entropy in the present GPT-2 setup
- token-level lead time exists in a conservative slice
- transition differs from the hallucination slice mainly by lower post-onset persistence

Not allowed:

- broad early-warning claim
- clean detector against transition
- cross-model generalization
- universal onset layer

## What remains unresolved

- whether persistence is only a useful discriminator or a deeper mechanism-level clue
- how much of the current picture is GPT-2-specific
- whether a small control model will preserve the onset / expansion / persistence structure
- whether feature-level decomposition can be meaningfully connected to the observed trajectory
  dynamics without collapsing into over-interpretation
